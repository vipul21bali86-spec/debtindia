How it hits the common man

This is where debt becomes personal

  • Taxes: A larger interest bill means more current revenue is used to pay for the past.
  • Jobs: If capex gets squeezed, roads, rail, housing, and related job creation can slow.
  • EMIs: Heavy government borrowing can keep financing conditions tighter for longer.
  • Hospitals and schools: More money to interest means less room for fresh social spending.
  • Crisis response: Lower fiscal space means less room to respond to shocks quickly.

India vs competitors

How other countries manage higher debt

Debt size alone does not decide risk. What matters is growth, who holds the debt, how transparent it is, and whether the government can refinance it safely.

India’s story is different from China, Japan, and the US. China’s challenge is local government and hidden debt, Japan relies heavily on domestic holders and the BOJ, and the US benefits from reserve-currency status. India’s advantage is growth and largely domestic financing, but it still needs discipline because fiscal room is limited.

What to watch

What India should learn from China and others

  • China: Avoid opaque local-government style debt build-up and off-balance-sheet financing.
  • Japan: High debt can be carried longer when financing is domestic, but that does not remove long-term risk.
  • US: Reserve-currency privilege gives more borrowing space, which India does not have.
  • India: Best protection is higher growth, cleaner fiscal math, and ensuring borrowing creates productive assets.
